ABOUT SETU

Waterford Institute of Technology (WIT), in collaboration with the newly established South East Technological University (SETU), is poised to play a significant role in driving innovation, fostering opportunities, and promoting growth in the Waterford region and beyond. As a leading higher education institution, WIT has a strong reputation for delivering exceptional learning experiences and cutting-edge research. Now, as part of SETU, WIT is uniquely positioned to leverage its expertise and resources to further enhance its impact on the local community and beyond. The establishment of SETU brings an exciting platform for WIT to thrive as a center for innovation. With a focus on collaboration, SETU brings together researchers, learners, and businesses to drive interdisciplinary initiatives and tackle real-world challenges. This collaborative approach fosters innovation, creating an environment where ideas are nurtured, and creativity flourishes.


SETU offers a wide range of exceptional learning opportunities across various fields, including science, engineering, business, humanities, health sciences, and more. Students benefit from state-of-the-art facilities, cutting-edge research, and a supportive learning environment that encourages critical thinking, creativity, and innovation. With a strong emphasis on practical learning and real-world applications, students gain valuable skills and knowledge that are highly relevant to the needs of today's job market.

HOW TO APPLY

You can apply for as many societies and sports via the SETU Societies and Sports page through a registration form. There is a application fee of €5.50 each if you are looking to join a Sports Team/Club and a fee of €3.50 for each society you want to join

BENEFITS TO JOINING SOCIETIES

Joining societies and sports clubs in SETU provides opportunities to meet like-minded individuals, develop skills, and enhance the overall college experience.


Sports clubs offer chances to stay active, develop teamwork skills, and create a great college/university spirit through organized sports and competitions.


Societies allow students to pursue interests, engage in extracurricular activities, and build networks while gaining valuable experiences.

HOW TO USE LIBRARY

During COVID-19 you would have to make a booking for what time you would like to start your library session but now you can walk in with your SETU student card on you.


If you want to borrow books students can borrow 8 long loan items for 2 weeks and students can borrow 2 short loan items for 1 week.


You can access SETU Waterford Libraries research resources 24/7 from anywhere. Off-campus access to library databases is available from the library database page using your SETU Waterford Network Login.

WELFARE AND EQUALITY

The union is dedicated to promoting the well-being of all students by providing a safe, inclusive, and supportive environment that fosters their personal and academic growth. This includes advocating for policies and programs that address issues such as mental health, housing, accessibility, and financial support, with the goal of ensuring that all students have equitable access to resources and opportunities. The SETU/WIT Student Union also actively promotes equality by actively addressing and challenging discrimination, bias, and inequality in all forms, including race, gender, sexuality, religion, and disability. Through its initiatives and campaigns, the union aims to create a campus culture that celebrates diversity, promotes inclusivity, and empowers students to thrive and succeed, regardless of their backgrounds or identities.


RAG WEEK/FRESHERS WEEK IN COLLEGE


Rag Week is a week long event that takes place around February and March the poster above is from rag week 2016 and lists out all the events available on campus and around the nightclubs and late night bars. Rag Week activities can include concerts, sports competitions, talent shows, themed parties, and other social events. Rag Week provides an opportunity for students to come together and have fun.


Freshers Week is a week long event just like Rag Week with events taking place both on campus and in the nightclubs and late night bars. Freshers week is usually organized at the beginning of the academic year and is focused on welcoming new students, commonly referred to as "freshers," to the university or college. It typically involves a series of events and activities designed to help new students get acquainted with the campus, meet other students, and learn about various student clubs, organizations, and resources available on campus.

RESIDENTIAL SERVICES

First, make sure to complete your documentation, including signing your Contract, Terms and Conditions, and Guarantor forms, which will be sent to you prior to check-in. Additionally, ensure that your payment is made in advance. There is a discount of €50 for accounts paid in full on or before the first day of the licence period (excluding Single Semester students). If you prefer to pay in instalments, the payment schedule is 55% on or before the first day of the licence agreement, 25% on or before November 3rd, 2023, and 20% on or before January 26th, 2024.

INFO FOR PARENTS


First and foremost, SETU student accommodation is located within the campus, providing a convenient and secure living option for students. The campus is well-maintained and monitored by security personnel, ensuring a safe environment for residents.


In addition, the accommodation facilities are equipped with various security features. This includes secure access control systems at the entrances, which are only accessible to residents with a valid key card or access code. CCTV cameras are also installed in common areas and outdoor spaces to enhance security measures.
