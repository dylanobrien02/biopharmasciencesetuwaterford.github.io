ABOUT WATERFORD

Waterford offers a unique blend of rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant modern lifestyle. As a college student, you'll find plenty of opportunities to thrive in Waterford, with its diverse population and welcoming community.


One of the highlights of Waterford is its rich cultural heritage, with landmarks such as the iconic Waterford Cathedral and the medieval Waterford Treasures Museum. The city also has a lively arts and music scene, with theaters, galleries, and music venues that cater to a wide range of interests. Waterford is also renowned for its crystal production, with the world-famous Waterford Crystal brand offering a unique cultural experience for residents and visitors alike.


In addition to its cultural richness, Waterford offers stunning natural scenery, including the scenic River Suir and the nearby Comeragh Mountains. Outdoor enthusiasts will find ample opportunities for hiking, cycling, and other recreational activities in the surrounding countryside. The city also offers a range of recreational facilities, parks, and sports clubs for those who enjoy an active lifestyle.


Waterford offers a unique and vibrant lifestyle for college students, with its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and welcoming community. If you're considering a move somewhere away from home, Waterford could be an exciting and fulfilling destination to pursue your studies and embrace new experiences.

With its rich history, strong sporting tradition, and community-oriented approach, Mount Sion GAA Club is a respected and cherished Club in Waterford.

WATERFORD GREENWAY



Previous Next



The Waterford Greenway is a popular recreational trail offering a scenic and enjoyable outdoor experience. Its a 46-kilometer cycling and walking path that follows the route of the old Waterford to Dungarvan railway line. One of the best ways to explore the Greenway is by renting a bike, and there are several bike hire options available along the trail. Bikes can be rented at various entry points, and there are options for both traditional bikes and electric bikes, making it accessible to a wide range of fitness levels and abilities. The trail is well-maintained and offers a smooth, easy-to-navigate surface, suitable for all ages and fitness levels. Along the route, there are plenty of rest areas, picnic spots, and scenic viewpoints to take in the beautiful countryside, including coastal stretches, fields, and charming villages. The Greenway also features informative panels that provide insights into the area's history, wildlife, and natural beauty, adding an educational aspect to the experience. Whether you're a local or a visitor to County Waterford, the Waterford Greenway offers a fantastic outdoor adventure, perfect for biking, walking, or simply enjoying the picturesque scenery..

ATTRACTIONS


MUESEUMS - REGINALD'S TOWER

Reginald's Tower is one of the city's most recognizable structures. This circular tower, made of limestone, dates back to the 12th century and is the oldest civic building in Ireland. It was originally built by the Vikings as a defensive structure and later served as a royal residence, a mint, and a prison. Today, Reginald's Tower is a museum that houses a collection of artifacts related to Waterford's Viking and medieval history, including weapons, jewelry, and coins. Visitors can explore the tower's various levels and enjoy panoramic views of the city and the River Suir from the top. Reginald's Tower is not only an important historical site but also a symbol of Waterford's rich cultural heritage, attracting tourists and history enthusiasts alike.

MUESEUMS - MEDIVAL MUSEUM

The Medival Museum is known for its rich collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the medieval history and heritage of the region. The museum is housed in a modern building that seamlessly blends with the historic surroundings, creating a unique architectural experience. Inside, visitors can explore a wide range of exhibits that include medieval weapons, armor, religious artifacts, and artwork. The museum provides a fascinating insight into the medieval period of Waterford, offering a glimpse into the lives, culture, and history of the people who lived during that time. With its extensive collection and informative displays, Medieval Museum Waterford is a must-visit destination for history buffs and anyone interested in the rich medieval heritage of Waterford City.

MUSEUMS - BISHOPS PALACE

Bishops Palace is a beautifully preserved Georgian mansion that dates back to the 18th century and is known for its impressive architecture and stunning interiors. The museum offers visitors a captivating journey through the history of Waterford, showcasing the life and times of the city's influential bishops and the social, cultural, and political aspects of the Georgian era. The museum features a rich collection of artifacts, including exquisite furniture, fine art, decorative objects, and historic documents, all carefully curated to provide a glimpse into the opulent lifestyle of the Georgian aristocracy. Visitors can explore the elegant rooms, admire the intricate details of the architecture, and learn about the significant events and personalities that shaped the history of Waterford City. Bishop's Palace Waterford is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts, architecture lovers, and those seeking to delve into the rich cultural heritage of Waterford.

Dungarvan is a coastal town located about 30 kilometers west of Waterford city, known for its scenic harbor and beautiful beaches. The town offers a range of activities, from strolling along the picturesque waterfront and marina to visiting historical sites like Dungarvan Castle, a 12th-century fortress. Dungarvan also has a vibrant food scene, with numerous pubs, cafes, and restaurants serving delicious local seafood and other culinary delights.

Dunmore Eastis a picturesque fishing village located on the coast of County Waterford, known for its stunning scenery and pristine beaches. The village is a popular destination for water sports enthusiasts, offering activities such as sailing, fishing, and swimming. You can also explore the historic lighthouse, take a scenic coastal walk, or simply relax and enjoy the serene atmosphere of this quaint village.

The Copper Coast is a stretch of coastline that extends from Kilfarrasy to Stradbally in County Waterford, known for its rugged cliffs, sandy coves, and unique geological formations. This UNESCO Global Geopark is a paradise for nature lovers, offering opportunities for hiking, rock climbing, and exploring the diverse flora and fauna of the area. You can also visit the Copper Coast Geopark Visitor Centre to learn about the area's rich geological and cultural heritage.
